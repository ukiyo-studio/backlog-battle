# Backlog Battle Pitch

Everyone has a backlog.

Movies you meant to watch. Games you bought on sale. Books sitting unread. Anime, TV shows, courses, albums, projects, and hobbies waiting for the mythical moment when you finally have time.

But when that time arrives, a different problem appears: decision fatigue.

Instead of choosing what to play, watch, read, or finish next, people freeze. The backlog feels too big, every option feels equally valid, and the easiest choice is to avoid choosing altogether. So they open TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, or X and start scrolling.

Backlog Battle turns that moment of indecision into a quick, playful decision-making experience.

The concept is simple: add your backlog, choose a category, and let your items battle it out in a tournament.

If you have 10 Steam games waiting to be played, Backlog Battle shuffles them into a knockout bracket. You are shown one matchup at a time: Game A vs Game B. Pick the one you would rather play. Winners advance, matchups continue, and by the end you have a ranked list that reflects what you actually want most right now.

Instead of manually sorting a giant list, you make small, easy choices.

Over time, Backlog Battle keeps your backlog alive by periodically prompting you to run new battles. Your interests change, your mood changes, and your rankings should change with them. A game that lost last month might win this month. A book you kept ignoring might suddenly rise to the top.

Backlog Battle helps people stop collecting endless options and start choosing what deserves their time next.

## The Problem

People do not only struggle with having too little time. They struggle with choosing how to spend the time they already have.

Modern entertainment creates endless personal backlogs:

- Games bought but never played.
- Movies saved but never watched.
- Shows started but never finished.
- Books purchased but never read.
- Anime, manga, courses, podcasts, hobbies, and projects waiting indefinitely.

Traditional backlog apps act like storage. They help people track what they have, but they do not help them decide what to do next.

Backlog Battle focuses on the decision.

## The Solution

Backlog Battle is a cross-platform app for iOS, Android, and Web that helps users prioritize their backlog through tournament-style battles.

Users create categories, add backlog items, and start a battle. The app generates matchups, the user picks winners, and the tournament produces an organic ranking.

The experience is fast, lightweight, and satisfying because users are never asked to rank everything at once. They only answer one question at a time:

Which one would you rather choose right now?

## Why It Works

Backlog Battle works because pairwise decisions are easier than full-list prioritization.

Ranking 50 games is hard. Choosing between two games is easy.

The tournament format makes prioritization feel like a game rather than a chore. It also creates a sense of progress and completion, giving users a clear answer at the end: this is what you should pick next.

## Example Use Case

A user has 10 games in their Steam backlog.

They open Backlog Battle, select their `Steam Games` category, and start a new battle.

The app creates matchups:

- Elden Ring vs Hades
- Cyberpunk 2077 vs Hollow Knight
- Baldur's Gate 3 vs Persona 5
- Stardew Valley vs Celeste
- Control vs Disco Elysium

The user picks winners one matchup at a time. After several rounds, Backlog Battle produces a ranked result showing which game they are most excited to play next.

The user did not need to think through the whole list. They just made a series of simple choices.

## Target Users

Backlog Battle is for people who collect more media than they consume and want a better way to choose what comes next.

Initial target users include:

- Gamers with large Steam, PlayStation, Xbox, Nintendo, or itch.io backlogs.
- Movie and TV fans with endless watchlists.
- Anime and manga fans tracking multiple series.
- Readers with growing book lists.
- Hobbyists juggling courses, projects, and creative goals.

## MVP

The first version should focus on the core loop:

- Create an account.
- Create backlog categories.
- Add backlog items manually.
- Start a battle from a category.
- Pick winners in head-to-head matchups.
- View the final ranking.
- Save battle history.
- Receive periodic reminders to battle again.

The MVP should avoid integrations and social features until the core experience proves useful and fun.

## Long-Term Vision

Backlog Battle can grow from a simple prioritization tool into a personal decision engine for entertainment and hobbies.

Future possibilities include:

- Steam, Goodreads, Letterboxd, Trakt, and other imports.
- Smarter ranking algorithms based on battle history.
- Mood-based battles, such as short, cozy, intense, social, or low-effort.
- Time-aware recommendations based on how much free time the user has.
- Seasonal backlog events and challenges.
- Shareable battle results.
- Community templates for common backlog types.

## One-Line Pitch

Backlog Battle helps people defeat decision fatigue by turning their backlog into quick, fun, tournament-style battles that reveal what they actually want to do next.
